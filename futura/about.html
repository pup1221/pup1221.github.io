About Futura Farms
Our Story
Founded in 2019, Futura Farms is located in the rural municipality of St Andrews, Manitoba - 25 minutes North of Winnipeg. Owned and operated by Carl Durand, Futura Farms is 37 acres in total and includes a 6 acre fenced in area where the majority of trees and shrubs are grown. Some of our deer resistant crops such as rhubarb and raspberries are grown outside the fenced area.

Futura Farms was developed to serve 3 separate needs. The first is to grow prairie fruit crops to supply the ever growing Manitoba food and drink market. The second is for large scale growing and collection of seeds to supply wholesale tree nurseries. Finally, in conjunction with Prairie Shade Consulting (PSC), we are testing new species of prairie-hardy trees and shrubs for future market introduction.

All aspects of the farm have been developed with environmental impacts in mind. We are 100% spray free. Solar panels on the roof of the barn will power all farm activities starting in summer 2020 Our long term goal is to have full electrification of all current machinery, and therefore, zero carbon emissions.

In conjunction with PSC, we are advancing the research and development of new tree and shrub species that can adapt to future climatic and pest adversities as we recognize the important roles urban forests play as carbon sinks as well as moderating urban air pollution. We also own and are preserving 6 acres of a 47 acre old growth Aspen-Oak parkland forest located at the west end of our property. We have plans to expand the forest through restorative tree plantings in the future.
